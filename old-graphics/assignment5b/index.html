Chalktalk: Survival Mode

By Iris Yuan

A suite of actions and environments to illustrate danger vs. safety.

Help show natural disasters, mental disorders, fight vs. flight, bullying, social behavior, and much more.

Survival Mode

Draw any character or living thing. It automatically has a sense of "perceived danger" and "actual danger".

Hurt the character by slashing across it with your mouse or tablet.

After being slashed, the character fades in/out (pulsing). It could change color.

Perceived Danger

The safer a character feels, the less likely they will fidget or evade from the mouse.

The more "danger" they perceive, the faster they run or move. The character can evade the mouse at certain speeds, or choose to stay and "fight" (illustrating fight vs. flight).

Wreak Havoc

Introduce danger onto the character's surroundings:

You could draw a lightning bolt, angry monsters by zigzagging the mouse, or have all objects on screen gravitate & encircle the current character to demonstrate bullying in school.

Bubble Protection

Demonstrate safety by drawing bubbles around character(s).

Draw a circle around multiple objects to show a safe community. Anyone outside that bubble could be percevied as "dangerous".

The "Big" Picture: Use Cases

  • fight vs. flight response
  • community & social groups ("us" vs "them")
  • civilization vs. anarchy
  • bullying
  • diseases
  • natural disasters
  • mental disorders
  • dreams vs. nightmares
  • emotions like fear & trust
